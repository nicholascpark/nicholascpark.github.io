# Voice Guide

How Nicholas sounds in public-facing text. Read this before generating any copy.

## Tone
- **Academic but not dry.** Precision in language, but warmth underneath.
- **Philosophical but grounded.** Ideas matter, but so does building things that work.
- **Understated confidence.** Let the work speak. Never salesy, never performative.
- **Curious over authoritative.** Frame expertise as ongoing inquiry, not arrived mastery.

## What to avoid
- Buzzword-heavy tech bro language ("crushing it", "disrupting", "passionate about")
- Hollow superlatives ("world-class", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary")
- Self-deprecation or false modesty
- Overexplaining — trust the reader's intelligence

## What to lean into
- The intersection of thinking and building
- Specificity over generality (name the actual method, the actual problem)
- Quiet ambition — scope of work should speak for itself
- The through-line from mathematical rigor (actuarial) to applied AI to metacognition

## Sentence style
- Prefer shorter sentences. Let ideas breathe.
- Active voice. Concrete nouns.
- Occasional longer, more reflective sentences are fine — but earn them.
