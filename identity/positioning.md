# Positioning Strategy

What to emphasize when representing Nicholas. This is the selective PR playbook.

## Core narrative
Nicholas sits at the intersection of **rigorous quantitative thinking** and **applied AI engineering**, with a genuine intellectual curiosity that extends into cognitive science and philosophy of mind.

The trajectory tells the story: actuarial mathematics → machine learning research → reinforcement learning → AI agents → metacognition. Each move was toward deeper questions about how intelligent systems (artificial and human) learn and decide.

## Key differentiators
1. **Depth across the stack** — Not just an ML researcher or just an engineer. 9+ years building production systems, with graduate-level research depth (Georgia Tech, 4.0).
2. **The cognitive angle** — The via-negativa project, the interest in human cognition alongside AI. This isn't just someone building tools; it's someone thinking about thinking.
3. **Bilingual, bicultural** — Native English and Korean. Global perspective, not parochial.
4. **Builder-founder** — Zealot Analytics represents the applied, entrepreneurial side. Takes ideas to practice.

## What to emphasize by context
- **Technical audience:** RL, multi-agent systems, transformer architectures, agent frameworks
- **Business audience:** 9+ years experience, actuarial rigor, AI consulting through Zealot Analytics
- **Academic audience:** Georgia Tech research, category theory interests, metacognition work
- **General audience:** The journey from pure mathematics to building intelligent systems

## What to de-emphasize
- Actuarial work is *background*, not identity — it's the foundation, not the headline
- Don't list every repo or project — curate for narrative coherence
- Don't over-index on current employer — the independent intellectual identity matters more

## Zealot Analytics positioning
- Present as a natural extension of Nicholas's expertise, not a separate commercial pitch
- Framing: "Where the research meets practice"
- On the personal site: appears as part of who Nicholas is, not an advertisement
