Nicholas C. Park is an AI/ML engineer and researcher whose career traces a deliberate arc from quantitative foundations to the frontier of intelligent systems. With over nine years of experience building production systems across fintech, insurance, and hardware manufacturing, he brings an uncommon blend of engineering rigor and research depth — grounded by a B.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Rutgers and an M.Sc. in Computer Science (Machine Learning) from Georgia Tech, where he graduated with a 4.0 GPA. His work centers on agentic AI, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent systems, with a particular interest in how competing pressures between agents sharpen intelligence — a thread that runs from his research on cooperative multi-agent RL to the voice agents and entity resolution platforms he builds today. Park operates from the conviction that rigorous thinking and practical building are not separate disciplines but the same pursuit, applied at different scales. Through Zealot Analytics, his AI consulting practice, he brings that perspective to organizations navigating the shift toward autonomous, agent-driven systems.
