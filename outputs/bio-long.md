Nicholas C. Park is an AI/ML engineer and researcher whose career traces a deliberate arc from quantitative foundations to the frontier of intelligent systems — beginning in hardware analytics and actuarial science, moving through machine learning engineering, and arriving at the design of autonomous AI agents. With over nine years of experience building production systems across fintech, insurance, and hardware manufacturing, he pairs applied engineering depth with graduate-level research in reinforcement learning and multi-agent systems, having earned his M.Sc. in Computer Science (Machine Learning) from Georgia Tech with a 4.0 GPA. His work sits at the intersection of software engineering, RL, and multi-agent AI, with a particular focus on agentic architectures using frameworks like LangGraph. Park's intellectual interests extend beyond building these systems to understanding how they learn — an inquiry that connects his research in cooperative multi-agent RL and adversarial NLP to a broader investigation of cognition, including subtraction as a lens for how minds refine knowledge. Through Zealot Analytics, his AI consulting practice, he brings this same orientation to client work: the conviction that rigorous thinking and practical building are not separate disciplines but the same pursuit. It is this through-line — from mathematical formalism to deployed intelligence — that defines both his engineering and his research.
